CYCO Groups

Previous( Section )     Next( Part )     Home     Contents     

A group is a means for treating collections of related parts with single operations. They are mostly used for muting and soloing groups of parts. Groups are always used in the context of a Section.

            
            section(a)
            
            (metronome a-metronome)
            (qball a-kick kick ...)
            (qball a-snare snare ...)
            (qball a-cymbal cymbal ...)
            (epart a-bass bass ...)
            (epart a-organ organ ...)
            
            (group a-percussion (list a-kick a-snare a-cymbal))
            (group a-instruments (list a-bass a-organ))
            
            (mute a-metronome   t)   ;; mute individual parts
            (mute a-kick      nil)
            (mute a-snare     nil)
            (mute a-cymbal    nil)
            (mute a-bass      nil)
            (mute a-organ     nil)
            
            (mute a-percussion   nil) ;; group level 
            (mute a-instruments  nil)
            
            (->midi a)

The group MUTE method takes a group object and a flag. If the flag is True all members of the group are muted. If the flag is nil the mute status of the individual members remains unchanged. This allows individual parts to be muted separate from the group to which they belong, without a later call to (mute <group> nil) clobbering things.

Use the UNMUTE method to unmute all members of a group. In practice unmute is rarely needed.

The other primary use for groups is to solo all member parts while simultaneously muting all non-member parts.

            
            
            (group a-percussion (list a-kick a-snare a-cymbal))
            
            (solo a-percussion)
